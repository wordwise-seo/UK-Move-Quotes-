Moving from Brighton to London

Brighton to Central London is around 55 miles — a well travelled route for removal companies covering East and West Sussex. The journey north on the A23 is generally straightforward outside of peak hours, though the approach into London can add significant time depending on traffic and which part of the capital you are heading to.

Brighton to South London neighbourhoods like Brixton, Clapham, Streatham, and Croydon is one of the most common versions of this move. Many people who have been living in Brighton make the move north for work or family reasons, and removal companies who do this route regularly know both cities well.

The complications at the Brighton end are the usual ones — parking suspensions if needed, staircase access in Victorian properties. The London end adds ULEZ charges and the possibility of congestion charge depending on where you are moving to. Ask your removal company to confirm how they handle these when you discuss the move.

Cost of a Brighton to London removal

A two bedroom property moving from Brighton to London typically costs between £700 and £1,200. A three or four bedroom house ranges from £1,000 to £1,800 or more depending on volume, access at both ends, and the specific London destination. ULEZ charges may add to the final cost.

Planning your move

What to think about for a Brighton to London removal

Start early from Brighton

An early start from Brighton gets the loading done before traffic builds and means you arrive in London with time on your side. Most removal companies doing this route suggest a 7am or 8am start from Brighton.

Check ULEZ and parking at the London end

ULEZ applies across most of Greater London. Many London streets also require parking bay suspensions for removal lorries. Your removal company should handle both — confirm this when you book.

Sort Brighton parking first

If your Brighton property is in a controlled parking zone, arrange the bay suspension with Brighton and Hove City Council at least five working days before your move date.
