Man with a van in Brighton — when it is the right choice

Brighton has an enormous demand for man with a van services. The city has one of the highest concentrations of flatshares and HMOs in the South East, a large student population moving in and out every September and June, and a constant stream of people moving between the city's Victorian terraces and modern developments.

For a studio flat, a one bedroom property, or a single room in a houseshare, a man with a van in Brighton is almost always the right choice and significantly cheaper than a full removal company. The same applies if you are moving a single item — a sofa, a bed, a chest of drawers — or collecting something large from a seller elsewhere in Brighton or the surrounding area.

Brighton man and van operators are used to the city's quirks. They know which streets have the worst parking, they know how to navigate the narrow lanes around the North Laine and the Hanover area, and they know that the seafront can get busy at weekends. That local knowledge saves time on move day.

Man and van prices in Brighton

Hourly rates for man with a van in Brighton typically run from £55 to £95 per hour depending on the operator, the size of vehicle, and whether a second person is included. Fixed price quotes are usually available for straightforward local jobs and often work out better value for the customer. A studio flat move within Brighton might cost between £130 and £280 in total depending on access and distance.

Tips for man and van moves in Brighton

Same day often available

Brighton has a large pool of man and van operators and same day bookings are often possible. Mention you need it quickly when you submit your request and operators with availability will respond.

Check parking in advance

Many Brighton streets have controlled parking. Your man and van driver will need somewhere to park close to both properties. Mention parking availability when you book so they can plan accordingly.

Victorian staircases

Brighton has a huge number of converted Victorian houses with steep, narrow stairs. Let your operator know about any staircase complications — they may need a second person or specialist equipment.
