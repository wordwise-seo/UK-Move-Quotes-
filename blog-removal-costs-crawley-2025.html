How much do removals cost in Crawley in 2025?

A straight talking guide to what you can realistically expect to pay for a house move in Crawley and the surrounding areas of West Sussex and Surrey.

One of the first things people want to know when they start planning a move is how much it is going to cost. The honest answer is that removal prices in Crawley vary quite a bit depending on a handful of factors, and anyone who gives you a firm number without knowing your specific situation is guessing.

What we can give you is a realistic picture of what people moving in and around Crawley are actually paying in 2025, based on typical jobs across the area. Use this as a starting point before you get your own quotes, not as a substitute for them.

Typical removal costs in Crawley by property size

Property size is the biggest single driver of removal costs. A studio flat takes a fraction of the time and resources of a four bedroom house, and that difference shows up clearly in the prices.

Property size Typical price range
Studio or 1 bed flat£250 to £500
2 bed property£400 to £750
3 bed house£600 to £1,100
4 bed house£900 to £1,500
5 bed or larger£1,400 upwards
Man with a van (hourly)£50 to £90 per hour

These figures are for local moves within Crawley or to nearby towns in West Sussex and Surrey. If you are moving further afield, say from Crawley to London or further north, you should expect to pay more because of the additional time and fuel involved.

Getting at least three quotes before you commit is the single most effective way to make sure you are paying a fair price. Prices for the same job can vary by several hundred pounds between companies, and the cheapest is not always the worst.

What pushes the price up

Beyond property size, a few things consistently add to the cost of a removal in Crawley.

Access at the property

This is the one that catches people out most often. A third floor flat with no lift and street parking 80 metres away is a much harder job than a ground floor house with a driveway. Removal companies factor in the time it takes to carry items to and from the van, and difficult access genuinely adds time to a job. If your property has any access complications, mention them upfront when you request quotes so companies can price accurately rather than adjusting on the day.

Time of year

Demand for removal companies in Crawley peaks in late spring and summer, particularly from May through to September. School holidays, especially the end of July and August, are the busiest weeks of the year. Prices do not always go up directly but availability gets tight, which means the companies with the best reputations get booked up early. If you can move between October and March you will generally have more choice and often find companies with more flexibility on price.

Move day

Fridays are the most popular moving day in the UK because conveyancing completions tend to happen on a Friday. The last Friday of each month is especially busy. If you have any flexibility in your moving date, even shifting to a Thursday can open up more options.

Packing services

If you pay a removal company to pack for you, expect to add anywhere from £150 to £400 to the overall cost depending on the size of your home. It is not something everyone needs but it is worth asking about, particularly if you are time-pressed or have a lot of fragile items.

What you can do to keep costs down

There are a few things that genuinely make a difference to what you end up paying.

Decluttering before you move reduces the volume of things that need to be transported. Less volume means a smaller van or fewer trips. People often know they should do this and do not get around to it, but it is one of the most effective ways to bring the cost down.

Packing your own boxes rather than paying for a packing service saves money if you are organised about it. Removal companies charge for the time their crew spends packing, so arriving on moving day with everything already boxed and labelled speeds the job up considerably.

Being flexible on your move date, as mentioned above, gives you more options. Even a day or two of flexibility can make a real difference to both availability and price.

Man with a van versus a full removal company

Not every move in Crawley needs a full removal company with a large lorry and a crew of three. For studio and one bedroom flats, single room moves, or situations where you just need help shifting a few large pieces of furniture, a man with a van is often all you need and considerably cheaper.

Man and van services in Crawley are typically priced either hourly or as a fixed price for the whole job. For short local moves a fixed price tends to work out better because you know exactly what you are paying before anyone turns up. Hourly pricing can creep up if the job takes longer than expected.

Getting quotes in Crawley

The best way to find out what your specific move will cost is to get quotes from local companies. Prices in any guide, including this one, are based on averages. Your actual quote will depend on the specifics of your situation.

When you get quotes, make sure you are comparing like for like. Some companies include packing materials as standard, others charge separately. Some carry goods-in-transit insurance as part of their price, others do not. Asking these questions before you book means no surprises later.
