Crawley to Croydon

Removals between Crawley and Croydon. A familiar route for local companies.

Crawley to Croydon is around 22 miles up the A23, a regular route for removal companies based in West Sussex. No congestion charge, straightforward roads, and companies who have done the journey many times.

Removals between Crawley and Croydon

Croydon is around 22 miles north of Crawley, closer than Central London and without the congestion charge zone. It is a popular destination for people moving out of Crawley who want to be closer to London, and equally popular for people leaving Croydon who want more space and a quieter setting.

The route between the two is straightforward, the A23 links them directly and most of the journey is on dual carriageway. Traffic is the main variable and removal companies doing this route regularly know which times of day work best.

Croydon has a mix of property types. New developments tend to have better parking and lift access than the older converted properties closer to the town centre. Let your removal company know about any access complications when you request quotes so they can plan accordingly.

Cost of a Crawley to Croydon removal

A two bedroom property can typically be moved between Crawley and Croydon for between £450 and £800. A three bedroom house usually falls between £650 and £1,100 depending on access and volume. ULEZ applies to most of Croydon so it is worth clarifying with your removal company whether this is included in their quote.

Good to know

What to know about moving between Crawley and Croydon

No congestion charge

Unlike Central London moves, Croydon sits outside the congestion charge zone. ULEZ still applies to most of Croydon so it is worth asking your removal company about this when you get your quotes.

Allow a full day

Loading, driving, and unloading between Crawley and Croydon typically takes a full working day for most property sizes. Plan for the crew to be with you from early morning through to late afternoon.

Check parking at the Croydon end

Croydon has controlled parking zones across much of the borough. If your new street has parking restrictions your removal company may need a suspension arranged with the council in advance.
