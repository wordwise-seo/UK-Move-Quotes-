Planning your move June 2025

When is the best time to move house in West Sussex?

Timing your move can make a real difference to what you pay, which companies are available, and how smoothly the day itself goes. Here is what the removal calendar in West Sussex actually looks like.

There is a question most people never think to ask when they start planning a house move and it can make a real difference to both cost and experience. When exactly should you do it? The answer in West Sussex is not as simple as picking a date that works for your solicitor. Demand for removal companies shifts significantly across the year, and knowing the pattern gives you a genuine advantage.

How demand changes across the year

The removal industry in West Sussex, like the rest of the UK, follows a reasonably predictable seasonal rhythm. Understanding it helps you get better availability, more choice of company, and sometimes a more competitive price.

January and February

Quietest months

The slowest period of the year for removals across Crawley and West Sussex. Fewer people move, which means removal companies have more availability and are often more willing to be flexible. If you can time a move here you will have the most choice.

March and April

Picking up

Spring brings the first wave of activity. The housing market gets busier after the post-Christmas slowdown and removal companies start filling their diaries. Still a decent time to move with reasonable availability if you book a few weeks ahead.

May to August

Peak season

The busiest period by a significant margin. Families with school-age children try to move during summer holidays, and the housing market tends to peak in late spring. Good removal companies in Crawley book up weeks in advance during this window.

September and October

Still busy

Activity stays relatively high through early autumn as people who planned summer moves finalise completions. October starts to ease off, giving you more options as the year winds down.

November and December

Getting quieter

Demand drops noticeably through November. December is particularly quiet as people avoid disrupting the Christmas period. If your move can happen in early December you may find very competitive availability, though it gets harder to arrange things in the final two weeks of the month.

Bank holidays

Avoid if possible

Bank holiday weekends see a spike in demand as people use the extra day to overlap their move. Prices can be higher and the best companies are often fully booked well in advance of popular bank holidays.

The day of the week matters too

Most people in West Sussex complete on a Friday. It has become so standard in the UK property market that removal companies often charge a small premium for Friday moves, and their Friday slots fill up weeks before their Monday to Thursday availability.

If your solicitor gives you any flexibility at all on the completion day, Thursday is worth asking about. You get the long weekend to unpack and settle in, removal companies have more availability, and the roads around Crawley tend to be slightly less chaotic in the afternoon than they are on a Friday when everyone is heading home for the weekend.

The last Friday of the month is consistently the single busiest day in the removal calendar across West Sussex. If your solicitor suggests that date, ask whether an earlier day in the same week is possible. It gives you more options and often a smoother experience.

What about the weather?

West Sussex weather is relatively mild compared to further north, but it is still worth thinking about. Moving in heavy rain with boxes getting wet on the path, mud being tracked through a new house, and everyone getting cold is genuinely unpleasant. Summer moves avoid the worst of that, which is partly why demand peaks then.

That said, a cold dry day in January is infinitely better for a move than a wet Friday in May. Weather should not be your primary factor but it is worth checking the forecast in the week leading up to your move and having a contingency plan for protecting cardboard boxes if rain is likely.

When is the genuinely best time to move in West Sussex?

If you have total flexibility, late September through to early November hits the sweet spot. The chaos of summer has passed, school holidays are over, the housing market is still ticking over, and removal companies in Crawley have availability without the January quiet that can feel a bit bleak if you are trying to get settled in a new home.

If you have no flexibility, that is fine too. The most important thing is booking your removal company as early as possible once you have an agreed completion date, getting at least two or three quotes, and being clear with your solicitor about any preferences you have on day of the week.
